About Fluid Medical

We started by protecting workers.
Now we're protecting health.

Fluid Medical grew out of a crisis — and a belief that technology built for the hardest problems can be turned toward the most human ones.

Our Story

Built in a crisis. Refined by purpose.

When COVID-19 hit, workplaces across Canada faced a question nobody had a good answer to: how do you know your people are safe to come in? Rapid testing was unreliable, contact tracing was slow, and the cost of getting it wrong was high.

Fluid Medical was born from that question. We built screening infrastructure that could protect workers at scale — fast, accurate, and privacy-respecting. That work taught us something important: when you build for the hardest environments, you learn how health data should actually be handled.

We applied those lessons to Wave. Not as a pivot, but as an evolution. The infrastructure we built for workplace health became the foundation for something much bigger — personal health intelligence that actually puts you in control.

In early 2026, we acquired Vessel Health — 42,000 users, seven patents, a $29.7M invested platform, and a team that shared our belief that health data belongs to the people it comes from. That acquisition became Wave.

2020
Founded during COVID-19

Fluid Medical launches workplace health screening infrastructure — rapid testing, contact tracing, and privacy-first employee health monitoring for remote and essential workers.

2021–2023
Sovereign data infrastructure

Parent company Fluid Scientific develops sovereign data platform principles — architecturally isolated data pods, consent-first design, and end-to-end encryption that became the foundation for Wave.

2024
Pivoting toward personal health

Fluid Medical expands its mandate from workplace screening to personal primary healthcare. Development of the Wave platform and the Fluid Precision urinalysis system begins.

Early 2026
Vessel Health acquisition

Fluid Medical acquires Vessel Health — bringing 42,000 users, 7 patents, and a $29.7M invested platform into the Wave ecosystem. Wave Mini prepares for Q3 2026 launch.

Q3 2026
Wave Mini launches

Wave Mini — free, no credit card — launches publicly. At-home urinalysis, wearable sync, and personal context in one sovereign health platform.

The Platform

Four pods, working together.

Wave brings together four data streams that no other platform connects — your diagnostics, your wearables, your personal context, and your records.

Pod 1
Fluid Precision

At-home urinalysis. 8 biomarkers. Lab-grade accuracy from your phone camera.

Pod 2
DailySignal

Wearable sync. Heart rate, HRV, sleep — in context, not in isolation.

Pod 3
Fluid+Me

Cycle tracking, symptoms, medications — the context that makes your data make sense.

Pod 4
My Records

AI-powered document reader for your labs, prescriptions, and clinical notes. Coming 2027.
