Why we exist

Health data should work for you.

Most health technology extracts value from users. It aggregates data, sells insights to insurers and advertisers, and leaves individuals with nothing but an app they paid for. We built Fluid Medical to invert that model entirely.

Our urinalysis platform — Oria — gives individuals access to the kind of biomarker tracking that previously required a clinical setting. A simple strip, scanned with your phone, returns results across up to 60 active markers. The data stays with you. It is encrypted, permissioned, and never sold.

At the same time, we built infrastructure that enables organisations — long-term care homes, employers, clinical research teams — to deploy population-level health monitoring without compromising individual sovereignty.

If your health data is being monetised by someone else, it is not YOUR health data.

That principle shapes every product decision we make: from the cryptographic architecture beneath Oria, to the contractual commitments we make to every user, to the deliberate choice to remain headquartered in Vancouver under Canadian data law — with regulated subsidiaries in the United States (Fluid Precision Ltd, Delaware) for CLIA-lab pathways and the UAE (Fluid Scientific FCZO) for MOHAP-compliant data residency.

What we stand for

Principles that are non-negotiable.

Data sovereignty

Your health data belongs to you. Not to us, not to an insurer, not to a platform. We built the infrastructure to make this promise enforceable, not just contractual.

Radical transparency

We publish our data architecture, our accuracy validation methodology, and our privacy commitments in full. No dark patterns. No fine print that contradicts the headline.

Access, not exclusivity

We build for people who have been underserved by health technology — not just the quantified-self early adopter. Oria's core tier is free because access to your own biomarkers should not be a luxury.
